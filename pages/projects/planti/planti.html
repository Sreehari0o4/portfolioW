Planti - Case Study

UX Researcher | UI Designer

Overview

This is a UX-focused case study for a mobile application aimed at helping users diagnose and care for their indoor plants. As part of the Google UX Design course, I followed a structured design thinking process to create an app that empathizes with the user and guides them toward better plant health — even without prior gardening knowledge.

Problem Statement

Many plant owners struggle to understand what’s wrong with their plants — yellowing leaves, wilting, or stunted growth. They either give up, over-care, or apply random fixes. The challenge was to design a digital solution that asks the right questions and offers friendly, personalized plant care guidance.

My Roles and Responsibilities

  • Conducted user interviews and surveys
  • Defined key user personas and pain points
  • Designed task flows and wireframes
  • Built a mobile prototype in Figma
  • Collected usability feedback and iterated

Tools & Technologies

RESEARCH PROCESS

1. Empathize – Understand the User

I began by understanding the everyday struggles of plant owners. Through surveys and short interviews, I collected insights into how users interact with their plants, what challenges they face, and how they currently try to solve issues.


Methods used:

  • Google Forms survey (30+ responses)
  • 1:1 interviews with 5 plant owners
  • Observational notes

Key findings:

  • Users were unsure if the plant's condition was due to sunlight, water, pests, or soil.
  • Most users lacked confidence in their decisions.
  • Many relied on guessing or trial and error.

2. Define – Identify Core Problems

I synthesized our research into a clear problem statement and user needs.


Problem Statement:

    “Users need a simple way to identify what’s wrong with their plants because they feel confused and overwhelmed by inconsistent or technical online advice.”

User Insight:

  • Users often describe their issue visually (e.g., “leaves turning brown” or “droopy stems”).
  • They prefer image-based recognition or guided forms over reading forums.

3. Ideate – Brainstorm Solutions

With the problem clearly defined, I brainstormed multiple ideas for potential solutions.


Ideas Explored:

  • Symptom checker with image upload
  • AI-based plant diagnosis
  • Chatbot with step-by-step troubleshooting
  • Plant care calendar/reminders
  • Visual suggestion cards

The most feasible and impactful solution inferred:

    A web-based interface that lets users input symptoms or upload a photo to get instant care suggestions.


To better understand how my idea fits into the user's day-to-day, I created a storyboard.

4. Prototype – Build the Experience

I developed user flows, a storyboard, and low- to high-fidelity wireframes to bring the idea to life.


User Flow Chart:


The user flow chart outlines the steps a user takes to interact with the system, from symptom input to receiving care suggestions.



Prototype Features:

  • Symptom input (text + dropdown)
  • Upload image of plant issue
  • View matching diagnosis + remedies
  • Plant care community chat




Persona:


Siramole – represents our target user's mindset and behavior.

5. Test – Get User Feedback

I conducted usability testing with some participants using the clickable Figma prototype.


Feedback Highlights:

  • Users appreciated the minimal and clean interface.
  • Suggestions were seen as helpful and “trustworthy”.
  • Visual tips were more effective than text-heavy ones.
  • Some users requested a plant care tracker/reminder feature.

Conclusion & Reflections

The Plant Care Diagnosis project was an insightful journey through the Design Thinking process, from understanding user frustrations to designing a practical, empathetic solution. By combining research, empathy, and iterative design, I was able to craft a prototype that supports users in identifying and caring for their struggling plants with ease and confidence.


This project helped me understand the value of:

  • Listening deeply to user needs
  • Prioritizing clarity and simplicity in design
  • Testing early to refine usability
